## The Goal Was Simple

One book a month. Twelve months. Twelve books. It sounded manageable when I wrote it into my planner in January. By March I was already behind.

That gap between plan and reality turned out to be the most interesting part of the whole experiment.

## What I Actually Read

I did finish twelve books — just not always in the month I intended. Some months I read three. Some months I read none and felt bad about it for two weeks before picking something up again.

The books ranged from fiction to management theory to memoir. The ones that stuck the longest were not the ones I expected. A short novel I grabbed on a whim stayed with me for months. A much-hyped business book I finished in three days and can barely remember.

## The Surprising Part

Reading more did not make me a faster reader. I am still slow. What changed was my tolerance for sitting still with a book — the willingness to stay in the chair even when my phone was nearby.

That patience transferred. I started finishing things I used to abandon. Documents I had been meaning to read. Long articles. Even this blog post, which I started drafting four times before sitting down and finishing it in one session.

## What I Would Do Differently

I would pick books with less obligation attached. Half my list was books I felt I *should* read. The ones I actually loved were the ones I chose on instinct — the ones where I read the first page in a bookshop and could not put them down.

Next year: fewer shoulds, more instinct.

## The One Thing That Helped Most

Keeping a book on the nightstand and not keeping my phone there. Not revolutionary advice. But it worked.
